About

An open experiment in
AI-assisted regional research

NortheastGenAI is the first workshop of its kind focused on Northeast India — a region home to over 200 languages, extraordinary biodiversity, and rich oral traditions that remain almost entirely absent from global AI research.

Inspired by Agents4Science 2025 (Stanford University), NortheastGenAI adapts the AI-authored workshop model for a domain where the stakes are highest: low-resource, underrepresented, and urgently underdocumented knowledge systems of the Eastern Himalayan and Indo-Burma region.

The workshop is explicitly experimental. We are not claiming AI research equals human scholarship. We are asking an open question — and publishing everything that results from asking it.

Submissions

Submission Guidelines

Please read carefully before submitting.

G1 AI must be the primary author. Papers must be substantially generated by an AI tool — Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, or equivalent. Human contributions are limited to prompting, structuring, and light formatting.
G2 Disclosure is mandatory. Every submission must include a short paragraph naming the AI model used and describing the prompting methodology. Submissions without disclosure will be desk-rejected.
G3 Maximum 4 pages, excluding references. Any standard academic format is acceptable (ACL, LREC, or similar).
G4 Northeast India focus required. All submissions must engage meaningfully with one of the five workshop tracks.
G5 Non-archival. Accepted papers may be submitted to other venues without restriction. All papers and AI-generated reviews are publicly visible on OpenReview.
G6 No fee. Submission, review, and attendance are completely free of charge.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be an academic to submit?
No. Anyone may submit — students, independent contributors, practitioners, and researchers. The requirement is engagement with Northeast India topics and transparent AI authorship.
What does "non-archival" mean in practice?
Authors are free to submit the same work to other conferences or journals. We publish an open-access proceedings PDF under CC-BY-4.0, but this does not constitute formal archival publication.
How are papers reviewed?
Each submission is reviewed by an AI review agent on four dimensions: relevance to NE India, factual plausibility, novelty, and clarity. MWire Labs organizers then perform a human editorial pass. All AI-generated reviews are published openly on OpenReview.
Is there a registration or submission fee?
No. NortheastGenAI 2025 is completely free — to submit, to attend, and to read.
Can I submit from outside Northeast India?
Yes, provided your paper engages meaningfully with one of the five tracks and maintains a clear NE India focus. The requirement is on the subject matter, not the author's location.
Why is this workshop non-archival?
Because it is explicitly experimental. The non-archival format lets us explore the question openly and publish transparently — without making claims about AI research quality that we cannot yet support.
