§ 01 · About

About the Workshop

An open experiment in AI-assisted regional research

NortheastGenAI 2025 is a non-archival, one-day virtual workshop in which AI agents are the primary authors and reviewers of short research notes. Human researchers contribute through prompt design, curation, and editorial oversight. All submissions, prompts, and AI-generated reviews are published openly on OpenReview.

The workshop is inspired by Agents4Science 2025 (Stanford University) and adapts the concept for a context that global AI research has largely overlooked: the languages, cultures, and knowledge systems of Northeast India — a region home to over 200 languages, exceptional biodiversity, and living oral traditions that remain almost entirely absent from mainstream AI corpora and benchmarks.

Can AI meaningfully contribute to documenting what makes Northeast India extraordinary? NortheastGenAI creates a transparent, openly documented sandbox to explore that question.

This is an experiment, not a claim. Non-archival status means authors are free to submit their work to other venues after the workshop. All proceedings are released under CC-BY-4.0.

§ 05 · Submit

Submission Guidelines

Submissions handled through OpenReview. Free account required.

R.1
AI as primary author. Papers must be substantially generated by an AI tool — Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, or equivalent. Humans contribute through prompting and light formatting only.
R.2
Mandatory disclosure. Every submission must include a disclosure paragraph stating which AI model was used and describing the prompting approach.
R.3
Length. Maximum 4 pages, excluding references. Shorter is acceptable.
R.4
Northeast India context required. All papers must engage substantively with one of the five topic tracks.
R.5
Non-archival. Accepted papers may be submitted elsewhere. All submissions and reviews are publicly visible.
